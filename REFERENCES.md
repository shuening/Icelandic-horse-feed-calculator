## NSC Guidelines for Metabolic Horses (10-12% threshold)

### Peer-Reviewed / Consensus Statements

1. **ECEIM Consensus Statement on Equine Metabolic Syndrome (2019)**
   - Recommends forage NSC <10-12% DM to minimize post-feeding glycemic and insulinemic responses
   - Based on Harris et al. recommendations
   - Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6524109/
   - Also: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6430910/

2. **Insulinemic Responses of ID Horses Offered Varying Levels of NSC (2025)**
   - Healthy horses and PSSM horses: forage <12% NSC DM results in reduced postprandial insulinemic responses
   - Notes that insulin-dysregulated (ID) horses may need stricter thresholds
   - Source: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0737080625002965

3. **Nutraceutical Supplement Mitigates Insulin Resistance in Horses (2024)**
   - Demonstrates that insulin sensitivity decreases in horses adapted to NSC-rich diets over time
   - Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11640219/

### Icelandic Horse-Specific Research

4. **Metabolic Impact of Weight Variations in Icelandic Horses (2021)**
   - Icelandic horses used as model for EMS/ID research due to breed predisposition
   - Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7847705/

5. **Weight Loss and Insulin Response in Icelandic Horses (2020)**
   - Weight loss linearly associated with reduction in insulin response
   - Longitudinal study over 1 year
   - Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7245939/

6. **Insulin Signaling in Insulin-Dysregulated Icelandic Horses (2023)**
   - Post-receptor alterations observed in high-insulin Icelandic horses
   - Source: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0739724023000383

## Forage NSC Comparisons (Timothy vs Teff vs Others)

7. **Fitting Teff into the Horse Diet — UMN Extension**
   - Teff: higher fiber, lower NSC than most cool-season grasses
   - Horses preferred timothy over teff but accepted teff after acclimation
   - Source: https://extension.umn.edu/horse-pastures-and-facilities/fitting-teff-horse-diet

8. **Teff Hay for Horses: Pros and Cons — The Horse**
   - Teff tends to have less NSC than timothy or alfalfa, but not always
   - Source: https://thehorse.com/160143/teff-hay-for-horses-pros-and-cons/

9. **Is Teff Grass Hay Always Low in NSC? — Kathryn Watts**
   - Teff NSC range: 7.2% to 15.9% (Dairy One lab analysis)
   - Emphasizes that individual batch testing is essential
   - Source: https://docslib.org/doc/522628/is-teff-grass-hay-always-low-in-nsc-by-kathryn-watts

10. **Low NSC Hay for Metabolic Horses — Anderson Hay**
    - Tested timothy hay can be excellent for metabolic horses
    - Teff frequently tests low but is harder to grow/harvest consistently
    - Source: https://www.andersonhay.com/blog/what-to-feed-horses-with-metabolic-issues-why-hay-testing-and-consistency-matter-more-than-species-alone

11. **Horse Hay: Understanding Sugar and Starch Content — KER**
    - Warm-season grasses (Bermuda, Teff) tend lower NSC than cool-season (Timothy, Orchard)
    - Source: https://ker.com/equinews/horse-hay-understanding-sugar-and-starch-content/

## Feeding Easy Keepers & Metabolic Horses

12. **Picking Hay for Sugar/Starch Sensitive Horses — KPP**
    - Metabolic horses: NSC around 10-12%
    - Normal horses can tolerate 20%+
    - Source: https://kppusa.com/picking-hay-sugarstarch-sensitive-horses/

13. **Icelandic Horse Breed Guide: Characteristics, Health & Nutrition — Mad Barn**
    - Icelandic horses are easy keepers, prone to EMS
    - Low-calorie forages ideal
    - Source: https://madbarn.com/icelandic-horse-breed-guide/

14. **Feeding Metabolic and Easy Keeper Horses — EquiManagement / ADM**

    - Source: https://equimanagement.com/resources/feeding-metabolic-and-easy-keeper-horses/

15. **Guide To Feeding Your Metabolic Horse — PetMD**

    - Source: https://www.petmd.com/horse/nutrition/feeding-metabolic-horses

## Soaking Hay to Reduce NSC

23. **General consensus across multiple sources:**
    - Soaking hay in cold water for 30-60 minutes can reduce water-soluble carbohydrates (WSC) by ~30%
    - Discard water after soaking — do not let horses drink it
    - Hot water leaches faster but also removes more nutrients
    - Avoid soaking >1 hour in warm weather (bacterial growth risk)
